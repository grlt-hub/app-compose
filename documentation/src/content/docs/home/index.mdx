## What it's for

<div class="lead">
  Features, services, and modules often know about each other directly. That makes them hard to test, reuse, and
  maintain. With App-Compose, each part declares what it needs, and you supply it — so you stay in control as your app
  grows.
</div>

## What you get

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Simplicity" icon="puzzle">
    A small API with zero dependencies: lightweight, no containers, providers, or decorators. Framework-agnostic.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Clarity" icon="sun">
    No magic, no globals. Context moves through clear, typed wiring. Your app runs exactly as you composed it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reusability" icon="random">
    Same code, different context per compose. Reuse across apps, tests, and environments — no copy-paste.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Testability" icon="approve-check-circle">
    Validate your composition in tests. Missing context, duplicates, and unused wires fail in CI — not at startup.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Observability" icon="magnifier">
    Inspect your app as plain JSON — log it, render it, diff it across environments. Hook into start, complete, and fail
    events for timing, logs, or traces.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>
